AI & Academic Research

Opening Questions for CUNY Libraries

We are here to open a conversation. 15‑minute snapshot.

Recap the poster: AI’s impact on academic research, our role in shaping it.

We’re not here to debate AI’s merits, but to discuss how libraries can adapt.

Why Now?

  • 100M + ChatGPT users
  • Publishers adding “AI study assistants”
  • AI tools integrated into research databases
  • Faculty (including Library Faculty) are seeking guidance and structure to understand AI
  • Students are using these tools willy-nilly, and need guidance on appropriate use

AI is mainstream. Ignoring it sidelines the library.

Two Disruptions We See

  • Full‑paper outsourcing
  • Shortcut searching / “AI summaries”

Examples: student submits ChatGPT draft; faculty relies solely on AI summaries.

Risks: academic integrity, incomplete scholarship.

Modeling & Teaching
Good Research

  • Demonstrate an AI‑integrated workflow—from idea to citation
  • Teach critical evaluation of AI output alongside traditional sources
  • Set transparent attribution & reproducibility standards
  • Champion academic integrity—assistive use vs. outsourcing

Our greatest value: showing what rigorous, AI‑assisted research looks like in practice and teaching students to replicate it.

Open Questions
for CUNY Libraries

  • What defines a “well‑documented” AI‑assisted project?
  • How do we embed that model into instruction sessions?
  • Could a shared rubric help evaluate AI use in student work?

Invite chiefs’ perspectives; these questions need collective thinking.

Possible Directions

Pilot • Draft Guidelines • Standards Working Group

  1. Pilot a research‑skills session that walks through the full AI‑aware workflow.
  2. Draft a concise guidelines document shareable across campuses.
  3. Convene a small working group to refine a rubric and collect success stories.

Engage
or Cede the Conversation

Re‑emphasize opportunity and invite collaboration. Thank the council and open floor for Q&A.
